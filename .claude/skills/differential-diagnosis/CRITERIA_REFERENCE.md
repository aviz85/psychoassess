# DSM-5 Criteria Reference Summary

## Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)

### Criterion A: Symptom Patterns

**Inattention (6+ symptoms for children, 5+ for adults)**
- Fails to give close attention / careless mistakes
- Difficulty sustaining attention
- Does not seem to listen when spoken to
- Fails to follow through on instructions
- Difficulty organizing tasks
- Avoids tasks requiring sustained mental effort
- Loses things necessary for tasks
- Easily distracted
- Forgetful in daily activities

**Hyperactivity-Impulsivity (6+ symptoms for children, 5+ for adults)**
- Fidgets or squirms
- Leaves seat when remaining seated expected
- Runs/climbs inappropriately
- Unable to play quietly
- "On the go" / "driven by motor"
- Talks excessively
- Blurts out answers
- Difficulty waiting turn
- Interrupts or intrudes

### Additional Criteria
- B: Several symptoms present before age 12
- C: Several symptoms in 2+ settings
- D: Clear evidence of interference with functioning
- E: Not better explained by another mental disorder

### Presentations
- **Combined**: Both inattention and hyperactivity-impulsivity criteria met
- **Predominantly Inattentive**: Only inattention criteria met
- **Predominantly Hyperactive-Impulsive**: Only H-I criteria met

## Specific Learning Disorder

### Criterion A: Difficulties in Learning (6+ months despite intervention)

At least one of:
1. Inaccurate or slow word reading
2. Difficulty understanding meaning of what is read
3. Difficulties with spelling
4. Difficulties with written expression
5. Difficulties mastering number sense, facts, or calculation
6. Difficulties with mathematical reasoning

### Additional Criteria
- B: Skills substantially below expected for age
- C: Begin during school-age years
- D: Not better explained by ID, sensory, other disorders, inadequate instruction

### Specify Area
- **With impairment in reading** (dyslexia): word reading accuracy, reading rate/fluency, reading comprehension
- **With impairment in written expression**: spelling, grammar, clarity/organization
- **With impairment in mathematics** (dyscalculia): number sense, math facts, calculation, math reasoning

### Specify Severity
- **Mild**: Some difficulty; may function with accommodations
- **Moderate**: Marked difficulty; requires intensive teaching
- **Severe**: Severe difficulty; requires ongoing intensive support

## Intellectual Disability

### Criterion A: Intellectual Deficits
Confirmed by clinical assessment AND standardized intelligence testing
- Reasoning, problem solving, planning, abstract thinking
- Learning from experience, academic learning

### Criterion B: Adaptive Functioning Deficits
Result in failure to meet developmental/sociocultural standards for:
- **Conceptual**: Language, reading, writing, math, reasoning, memory
- **Social**: Empathy, social judgment, communication, friendships
- **Practical**: Self-care, job responsibilities, money, recreation, health

### Criterion C: Onset During Developmental Period

### Severity Levels (based on adaptive functioning)
- **Mild**: May be indistinguishable in early years; academic support needed; independent living with support
- **Moderate**: Language, academic, self-care lag behind peers; significant support in work/living
- **Severe**: Limited language; requires support for all ADLs; supervision needed
- **Profound**: Very limited communication; dependent for all aspects of care

## Autism Spectrum Disorder

### Criterion A: Social Communication/Interaction Deficits (all 3)
1. Deficits in social-emotional reciprocity
2. Deficits in nonverbal communicative behaviors
3. Deficits in developing/maintaining relationships

### Criterion B: Restricted, Repetitive Behaviors (2+ of 4)
1. Stereotyped or repetitive motor movements, use of objects, or speech
2. Insistence on sameness, inflexible adherence to routines
3. Highly restricted, fixated interests (abnormal in intensity or focus)
4. Hyper- or hyporeactivity to sensory input

### Additional Criteria
- C: Symptoms present in early developmental period
- D: Cause clinically significant impairment
- E: Not better explained by ID or global developmental delay

### Specifiers
- With/without intellectual impairment
- With/without language impairment
- Associated with known medical/genetic condition
- Associated with another neurodevelopmental/mental/behavioral disorder
- With catatonia

### Severity Levels
- Level 1: Requiring support
- Level 2: Requiring substantial support
- Level 3: Requiring very substantial support

## Generalized Anxiety Disorder

### Criterion A
Excessive anxiety and worry about multiple events/activities, occurring more days than not for 6+ months

### Criterion B
Difficulty controlling worry

### Criterion C (3+ symptoms, children need only 1)
- Restlessness
- Easily fatigued
- Difficulty concentrating
- Irritability
- Muscle tension
- Sleep disturbance

### Additional Criteria
- D: Causes significant distress/impairment
- E: Not due to substances or medical condition
- F: Not better explained by another mental disorder

---

## Social Anxiety Disorder

### Criterion A
Marked fear or anxiety about social situations where scrutiny possible

### Criterion B
Fear of acting in way that will be negatively evaluated

### Criterion C
Social situations almost always provoke fear/anxiety

### Criterion D
Situations are avoided or endured with intense anxiety

### Criterion E
Fear is out of proportion to actual threat

### Criterion F
Duration 6+ months

### Additional Criteria
- G: Causes significant distress/impairment
- H: Not due to substances or medical condition
- I: Not better explained by another disorder
- J: If another medical condition present, fear is unrelated

---

## Major Depressive Disorder

### Criterion A (5+ symptoms, 2+ weeks, including 1 or 2)

1. **Depressed mood** (in children: can be irritable)
2. **Diminished interest or pleasure** (anhedonia)
3. Significant weight/appetite change
4. Insomnia or hypersomnia
5. Psychomotor agitation or retardation
6. Fatigue or loss of energy
7. Feelings of worthlessness or excessive guilt
8. Diminished concentration or indecisiveness
9. Recurrent thoughts of death/suicidal ideation

### Additional Criteria
- B: Causes significant distress/impairment
- C: Not due to substances or medical condition
- D: Not better explained by other disorders
- E: No history of manic/hypomanic episode

---

## Oppositional Defiant Disorder

### Criterion A (4+ symptoms, 6+ months, with non-sibling)

**Angry/Irritable Mood**
- Often loses temper
- Often touchy or easily annoyed
- Often angry and resentful

**Argumentative/Defiant Behavior**
- Often argues with authority figures
- Often actively defies or refuses to comply
- Often deliberately annoys others
- Often blames others for mistakes

**Vindictiveness**
- Has been spiteful or vindictive at least twice in 6 months

### Additional Criteria
- B: Associated with distress or impairment
- C: Not exclusively during psychotic, mood, or substance disorder
- D: Not meeting criteria for Conduct Disorder or ASPD (if 18+)

### Severity
- Mild: One setting
- Moderate: Two settings
- Severe: Three or more settings

## Educational Classification: Emotional Disturbance

*(IDEA category, not DSM diagnosis)*

### Definition
A condition exhibiting one or more of the following over a long period of time and to a marked degree that adversely affects educational performance:

1. Inability to learn that cannot be explained by intellectual, sensory, or health factors
2. Inability to build or maintain satisfactory interpersonal relationships
3. Inappropriate types of behavior or feelings under normal circumstances
4. General pervasive mood of unhappiness or depression
5. Tendency to develop physical symptoms or fears associated with personal or school problems

*Includes schizophrenia. Does not apply to children who are socially maladjusted unless also meeting ED criteria.*
